A Stealthy Backdoor Attack on Speaker Recognition via Phase-Injection Hidden Triggers

Abstract

The development of deep learning has seen significant breakthroughs in speaker recognition, driven by the continual advancement of big models and big data. However, this progress has also brought attention to the security issues surrounding deep learning. Due to the constraint in data and computing resources, the training resources provided by third-party platforms have become a valuable asset. Unfortunately, malicious third-party platforms have introduced a severe security concern backdoor attacks, in which attackers can force a model to output a pre-defined label through a trigger. Existing speech backdoor attack methods typically employ fixed and unnoticeable perturbations as the trigger. However, it can be audible. Leveraging the fact that the human ear is not sensitive to phase information and that injecting extensive perturbations in the frequency domain can spread to small perturbations across the entire time domain, we propose a stealthy backdoor attack that injects triggers from the phase spectrum of the frequency domain, which is inaudible and imperceptible. Extensive experiments on the Voxceleb1 datasets demonstrate the effectiveness and stealthiness of our attack method, which can resist fixable defense methods.
